What an author fiche contains

Each author fiche follows a fixed matrix, so that Tom Sharpe, Wodehouse or Muriel Spark can be read through comparable landmarks.

  • Bio · life strata – neutral, sourced biographical layers;
  • Author’s note – a signed billet by The Novel of Comedy;
  • Comedy & currents – position in the atlas;
  • Style & comic palette – language, rhythm, scene work;
  • Life & workshop – biography as toolbox;
  • How they wrote – habits, rituals, notebooks;
  • Humorous friendships & influences – peers, mentors, heirs;
  • Selection – novels followed in the atlas;
  • Reception & reputation;
  • Conclusion – why this author in the atlas? + a small data stub.
Method

Author fiches and novel fiches

Novel fiches remain the core of The Novel of Comedy. Author fiches bundle together how a writer deploys comedy across several works, without turning into a full biography or a moral tribunal.

Author fiches · Role in the atlas

An author fiche gathers what concerns the practice of comedy: through-lines over a career, recurring motifs, favourite settings, relation to language and society.

It does not aim to say everything about a life, but to build a usable compass: where does this writer sit in the three currents? What kinds of comic engines do they revisit?

The focus stays on how comedy works in the books, not on judging the life itself.

Bio capsule and comic workshop

When sources allow, each fiche opens with a bio capsule (“Bio · life strata”): childhood, education, professions, exiles, returns, recognitions.

This neutral layer can be expanded over time as new documents appear. The rest of the fiche then reads these strata as a workshop: what kinds of institutions, landscapes or shocks feed the comic devices?

The question “must we separate work and author?” often remains abstract. Here, the bio capsule and the comic workshop are simply articulated, not fused.

Matrix

A ten-chapter matrix for authors

Like novel fiches, author fiches follow a fixed architecture. Ten chapters, plus a small data stub, so that comparisons remain possible from one writer to another.

Chapters of an author fiche
  1. Bio · Life strata – neutral, sourced biographical layers; main dates and environments.
  2. Author’s note – a short, subjective billet by The Novel of Comedy.
  3. Comedy & currents – how the author sits in the three currents; dominant branches and Labs.
  4. Style & comic palette – prose, dialogue, scene grammar, comic palette.
  5. Life & workshop – biographical experiences turned into comic mechanisms.
  6. How they wrote · Habits & rituals – routines, revisions, notebooks, workspaces.
  7. Humorous friendships & influences – predecessors, peers, heirs, cross-arts links.
  8. Selection · Works in the atlas – novels for which full novel fiches exist.
  9. Reception & reputation – contemporary and later readings, debates, criticisms.
  10. Conclusion · Why in the atlas? – what this writer calibrates for the map of comedy.

Each author fiche ends with a short data stub (JSON-LD style) summarising coordinates: dates, country, currents, branches, Labs. This technical layer serves catalogues, libraries and AI assistants.

Tom Sharpe
1928–2013 · United Kingdom · English
Core Comedy Comedy of Everyday Comedy of Crime Midlands Lab
Brilliant losers, collapsing institutions and farces that turn frustration into a comic engine. A reference point for Frustration Comedy and for the link between Everyday and Crime currents.
Fiche available · full matrix
P. G. Wodehouse
1881–1975 · United Kingdom / US · English
Core Comedy Comedy of Everyday Code/Etiquette London Elite Lab
Clubs, aunts, country houses and Jeeves: a central figure for the comedy of codes, wit and the etiquette of chaos in 20th-century fiction.
Fiche in progress · matrix being filled
Muriel Spark
1918–2006 · United Kingdom · English
Hybrid / Transversal Comedy of Everyday Edge Urban Lab
Grace and cruelty, comedy on the edge of catastrophe. A key voice for the thin line between lightness and moral vertigo.
Fiche in progress · planned
Percival Everett
1956– · United States · English
Hybrid / Transversal Neo-Nonsense Minor-Character Turn US South Lab
From pastiche to radical rewritings, a major contemporary figure for comic recentering of minor characters and postmodern Neo-Nonsense.
Fiche in progress · planned
